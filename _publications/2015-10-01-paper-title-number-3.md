We analyze the internal representations of large language models and identify a low-dimensional hyperplane that consistently separates truthful from untruthful statements across different models and domains. This finding provides a geometric interpretation of truthfulness in LLMs and enables simple intervention techniques to improve truthfulness without fine-tuning.
