Hallucinations in large language models pose significant challenges for safe deployment. This work introduces a novel detection and mitigation approach based on the sharpness of internal representations across in-context examples. By monitoring representation geometry, we can identify potential hallucination triggers and apply corrective interventions, reducing hallucination rates across diverse tasks.
